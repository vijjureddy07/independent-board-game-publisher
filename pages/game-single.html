Citadels of the Deep drops players into a world of contested coastlines, where every bay and headland is a prize worth fighting for. You'll build fortresses, command fleets, and negotiate — or betray — the alliances that keep you afloat.

The game unfolds across three Acts. In Act One, players scramble to claim uncontested territory. Act Two introduces the Tide Mechanic — a rotating pressure system that floods low-lying fortifications and forces strategic retreats. By Act Three, the deep-water citadels dominate the board and only the strongest network of alliances survives.

Every player board is asymmetric. The five factions — Ironmarch, Saltkin, Veilborn, Coralheld, and the Tidewraith — each have a unique action track, starting resources, and one-time power card that can swing a game decisively when played at the right moment.

Design intentions

Lead designer David Osei built the core loop around one question: "what if the most important move is the one you don't make?" Every action advances you — but also gives opponents information. The best players learn to be deliberately slow.

The game supports 2–5 players with no added rules complexity. Two-player plays as a direct duel with an automated pressure system; five-player introduces full coalition politics.
