Our Story

Built for game designers.

TabletopForge started as a tool we built for ourselves — a place to design, prototype, and publish independent board games without needing a factory, a publisher, or a middleman.

Founded 2018

We built the tools
we wished existed.

In 2018, four designers sat around a table with a prototype, a camera, and a frustrating question: why is it so hard for an independent designer to print, playtest, and sell a board game? The tools were scattered, expensive, or built for factories — not for individuals.

So we built TabletopForge — a single platform where any designer can upload artwork, order a physical prototype, run online playtesting sessions, and list their finished game for sale. No manufacturing contacts needed. No middlemen.

Today, over 3,500 independent designers use our platform. We've helped publish 12,000+ titles, shipped to 48 countries, and run hundreds of thousands of online playtest sessions. We answer to our designer community — every feature we build comes directly from their feedback.

Our Mission

What we stand for.

01

Designers first

Every feature we build starts with a designer's problem. Not what's easy to build — what actually helps someone take their game from idea to shelf.

02

No gatekeepers

You shouldn't need a publisher, a factory connection, or a six-figure budget to make your game. We remove every barrier between a great idea and a finished product.

03

Community as collaborators

Designer feedback shapes every feature we build. Forum suggestions have become platform tools. You're not just a user — you're building the future of indie game publishing.

04

Sustainable by design

We print regionally where possible, use FSC-certified materials, and offset the carbon footprint of every fulfilment run.

05

Accessible to everyone

From print-and-play files to colour-blind-safe component design, we build games that as many people as possible can actually play.

06

Fully independent

No investors, no licence obligations, no brand deals. Our only obligation is to make great games and treat the people who fund them with respect.

Since 2018

Our journey so far.

2018

TabletopForge platform launches

Four designers build the first version to solve their own problem — how to get a physical prototype without a factory contact. 200 designers sign up in the first month.

2019

Print-on-demand goes live

Any designer can now upload artwork and order a physical prototype shipped to their door. First 1,000 prototypes printed. Minimum order: one copy.

2020

Online playtest rooms launch

Pandemic or not, playtesting never stopped. Our virtual tabletop engine let designers run sessions with players worldwide — 50,000 sessions in the first year.

2022

Designer Marketplace opens

Designers can now list their finished games for sale directly through the platform. We handle print-on-demand fulfilment, payments, and shipping to 48 countries.

2024

Crowd Campaigns feature launches

Designers can run fully integrated crowd campaigns — with funding goals, stretch goals, and supporter tiers — all inside the platform. No third-party needed.

2026

12,000+ games published. 3,500+ designers.

TabletopForge is now the home for independent board game design. From napkin sketch to worldwide shelf — we handle every step in between.
